About Alfaisal

Two decades of building Saudi Arabia's academic future.

Our Story

Founded in 2002, on royal grounds, for a national purpose.

Alfaisal University was established under the patronage of the family of the late King Faisal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud, on land that once formed part of the royal estate in Riyadh. What began as a bold educational initiative has grown into one of the Kingdom's most respected non-profit research universities.

Since welcoming its inaugural cohort in 2008, Alfaisal has built a reputation for rigorous, NCAAA-accredited programs across six colleges — Business, Engineering & Advanced Computing, Medicine, Pharmacy, Science & General Studies, and Law & International Relations — supported by partnerships with organizations such as Boeing, Shell and King Faisal Specialist Hospital & Research Centre.

Vision & Mission

What drives everything we do

01

Our Vision

To be a leading research university in the region — recognized globally for the quality of its graduates, the impact of its research, and its contribution to the Kingdom's knowledge economy.

02

Our Mission

To educate future leaders through a rigorous, student-centered curriculum; to advance knowledge through research and innovation; and to serve the community in support of Saudi Vision 2030.

Milestones

Our journey so far

2002

Alfaisal University is founded

Established under royal patronage on historic grounds in Riyadh.

2008

Inaugural student cohort arrives

The College of Business, Engineering and Science welcome their first students.

2012–2018

NCAAA accreditation & expansion

College of Medicine and College of Pharmacy launch; national accreditation secured across programs.

2019–2024

Research recognition grows

Fourteen Alfaisal faculty named among Stanford's World's Top 2% Scientists.

Today

7,000+ students, 54+ nationalities

A thriving research university aligned with Saudi Vision 2030.
